Ten fun Christmas facts!

  1. Finding a spider or a spider’s web on a Christmas tree is believed to be a harbinger of good luck in Germany, Poland and Ukraine. source
  2. It is a custom in Japan to eat at KFC for Christmas dinner.source
  3. In Germany, Christmas Eve is thought to be a magical time when the pure of heart can hear animals talking.source
  4. The tradition of putting tangerines in stockings comes from 12th-century French nuns who left socks full of fruit, nuts and tangerines at the houses of the poor.source
  5. The image of Santa Claus being a thick-bellied Dutch sailor in a green winter coat was created by Washington Irving, the same author who dreamt up the Headless Horseman.source
  6. For a christmas to be officially classified as “white” a single snow flake needs to be observed falling in the 24 hours of 25th December on the rooftop of the Met Office HQ in London.source
  7. The Star of Bethlehem - the one the wise men followed to find the little baby Jesus - was probably a comet.source
  8. Many parts of the Christmas tree can actually be eaten, with the needles being a good source of Vitamin C.source
  9. On average, one to three Christmas trees are planted to replace each one harvested.source
  10. In 2008, residents of the state of Maine in America built the world’s biggest ever snowman. He stood at 122ft and 1inch tall.source
