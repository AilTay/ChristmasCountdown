Panettone recipe:

What you will need:

  • 25g fresh yeast
  • 200ml tepid whole milk
  • 400g white bread flour
  • 1tsp salt
  • 50g caster sugar
  • 115g softened, unsalted butter
  • 2 medium egg yolks
  • 1tsp vanilla extract
  • 75g candied citrus peel, finely chopped
  • 75g golden sultanas
  • pinch of grated nutmeg
  • 2 tsp icing sugar, to dust

Method:

  1. Crumble yeast into small glass bowl and add the tepid milk. Mix the yeast until it dissolves and forms a smooth paste.
  2. Mix flour, salt and sugar in a large mixing bowl and make a well in the centre.
  3. Pour the yeast and milk mixture into the centre of the well and mix gently with a small amount of the flour mixture to form a smooth paste.
  4. Cover with a clean tea towel and leave at room temperature for 20 minutes until the paste forms a 'sponge' and is frothy and bubbling.
  5. Mix in the rest of the flour around the sides to form a stiff dough.
  6. Kneed the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  7. Put the dough into a lightly oiled glass, china or plastic bowl which is big enough to let the dough double in size.
  8. Cover with a tea towel and leave to rise in cool room for about 1 and 1/2 to 2 hours, until doubled in size.
  9. Once it has risen, knock back and form it into a ball. Then let it rest for 5 minutes.
  10. Grease and line a deap 20cm round cake tin.
  11. Lightly dust work surface with a little flour and flatten the dough slightly.
  12. Spread butter over the top of the dough, put the egg yolks and vanilla extract in the middle and pile the fruit and nutmeg on top.
  13. Fold the sides of the dough over and carefully need until well blended. The mixture will be sticky until the ingredients are well mixed.
  14. Form into a ball by tucking outer dough into the centre and rotating in your hands. Then lower into the tin seam side down.
  15. Cover loosely with oiled clingfilm and set aside in a warm place for about an hour, until the middle of the dough is near the top of the tin.
  16. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 200 degrees C.
  17. Cut a cross in the centre of the dough using a sharp knife about 1cm deep.
  18. Bake in the centre of the oven for 10 minutes then reduce the heat to 180 degrees C and bake for 45 minutes until golden and risen.
  19. Leave to cool then dust with icing sugar.
